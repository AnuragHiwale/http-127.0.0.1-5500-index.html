ASTON MARTIN

Here's the information about ASTON MARTIN DB12

Aston Martin Lagonda Global Holdings PLC is a British manufacturer of luxury sports cars and grand tourers. Its predecessor was founded in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford. Steered from 1947 by David Brown, it became associated with expensive grand touring cars in the 1950s and 1960s, and with the fictional character James Bond following his use of a DB5 model in the 1964 film Goldfinger. Their grand tourers and sports cars are regarded as a British cultural icon

The Aston Martin DB12 is a luxury grand tourer that represents a significant evolution in the brand's designand engineering. Launched as a successor to the DB11, the DB12 features a bold and aggressive exterior with a wide front grille, sharp LED headlights, and sleek, aerodynamic lines. Under the hood, it boasts a powerful twin-turbo V8 engine that delivers impressive performance, blending speed with refined handling. The interior isequally luxurious, offering premium materials, advanced technology, and a driver-focused cockpit. The DB12 combines Aston Martin's signature elegance with modern performance, making it a standout in the grand touring segment.

  2. 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine.
  3. Produces 671 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque.
  4. 0 to 60 mph in approximately 3.5 seconds.
  5. Top speed of around 202 mph

The Aston Martin DB12 takes the legacy of the DB series to new heights, positioning itself as a "Super Tourer" that marries luxury, performance, and cutting-edge technology. It’s powered by a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine, producing around 671 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.5 seconds. The DB12 also features an advanced suspension system with adaptive dampers, providing a balance between comfort and dynamic handling, making it suitable for both spirited drives and long-distance cruising. Visually, the DB12 is striking, with a more muscular stance than its predecessor. The wider track, larger grille, and sculpted bodywork not only enhance its aesthetic appeal but also improve aerodynamics and cooling efficiency. The car sits on 21-inch alloy wheels, further accentuating its sporty character. Inside, the DB12 offers a mix of tradition and modernity. The cabin is wrapped in hand-stitched leather, Alcantara, and carbon fiber, creating an environment of opulence. A new infotainment system with a 10.25-inch touchscreen takes center stage, featuring customizable displays, seamless smartphone integration, and a premium audio system. The DB12 also includes advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) for enhanced safety and convenience. In essence, the Aston Martin DB12 is more than just a car—it's a statement of sophistication, combining Aston Martin's heritage with the latest in automotive technology to deliver a driving experience that is both thrilling and luxurious.
